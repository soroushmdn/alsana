What are petrochemical products ?

Petrochemical products are chemicals and polymers derived from oil or natural gas. They are an important part of the chemical and polymer industries because the demand for plastic and synthetic materials is constantly growing and on the other hand they have a major share in the economy and society today. These products are used to produce thousands of different products that people use dail


Petrochemical products are extremely versatile and come in a wide range of products, from food to basic chemicals, chemical intermediates (derivatives) and finally end products including plastics, rubber, resins, synthetic fibers, adhesives, paints, materials. Detergents include pesticides. In the petrochemical industry, the most abundant organic chemicals produced are methanol, ethylene, propylene, butadiene, benzene, toluene, and xylene

Division of petrochemical industries

  • Upstream units: In oil and gas refineries, the saturated or unsaturated linear hydrocarbons in oil or gas are separated from the rest of the hydrocarbons and sent as feed to the base units.
  • Stem units: In olefins, aromatics, and chemical complexes, raw materials are converted to base materials such as ethylene and propylene for intermediate units.
  • Intermediate units: Intermediate units: From base materials, they produce the feed of the final units.
  • Final units: In these units, end-of-chain products are produced and marketed.
  • Downstream units: In these units, petrochemical end products are converted into consumer products such as synthetic fibers, plastics, rubber, and so on.

  • Petrochemical products are generally divided into 3 categories:

  • Basic products: include ethylene, propylene, sulfur, gasoline, ammonia and , which form the basis of production in petrochemicals.

  • Intermediate products: Intermediate products: This category converts basic products into feed needed to produce final products, such as PVC, ethylene glycol, melamine and...

  • Final products: Used to make tools and equipment used by industry and the general public; Such as synthetic fibers, rubber, plastic devices, chemical fertilizers, medical devices and...

Petrochemical Special Economic Zone

Pars Energy Special Economic Zone (Assaluyeh)

During the past years, 13 petrochemical production complexes in this region have been put into operation. It is predicted that in the coming years, with the commissioning of other phases of the South Pars refinery and the supply of feed required by the petrochemical industry, 26 more petrochemical projects will be put into operation in this region.

Petrochemical Special Economic Zone (Mahshahr)

Petrochemical Special Economic Zone is located in the southwestern part of Iran and on the shores of the Persian Gulf, located in Mahshahr city, Bandaram Khomeini section.

At present, 21 production complexes have been designed and constructed in the Petrochemical Special Economic Zone. At present, there is about 40% of the country’s petrochemical production capacity in this region. According to the plans, 8 more petrochemical projects are expected to be put into operation in this area in the coming years.
